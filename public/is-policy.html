1. Purpose and Scope

This Information Security Policy ("Policy") establishes the security objectives, principles, and responsibilities for Project Sovereign ("the Platform"), an AI-powered UK M&A deal intelligence system operated by Howard Henry.

This Policy applies to:

  • All systems, services, and data operated by or on behalf of Project Sovereign
  • All third-party service providers with access to Platform data (Vercel, Supabase, Stripe, Anthropic, Resend, Twilio)
  • All current and future personnel with administrative access to the Platform

This Policy is reviewed annually or following any significant security incident, material change to the Platform, or change in applicable law.

2. Information Security Objectives

Project Sovereign is committed to protecting the confidentiality, integrity, and availability (CIA) of all information assets. The Platform shall:

  • Confidentiality: Ensure that deal data, personal data, and financial information are accessible only to authorised users. All personal data is processed in accordance with UK GDPR / DPA 2018.
  • Integrity: Prevent unauthorised modification of data. All mutations are logged to the immutable audit_trail table.
  • Availability: Maintain service availability with a Recovery Time Objective (RTO) of 4 hours and Recovery Point Objective (RPO) of 1 hour for critical systems.

5. Access Control

5.1 Principle of Least Privilege

All users are granted the minimum access required to perform their function. The Platform enforces three roles: user, admin, and superadmin. Row-Level Security (RLS) is enforced on all 28 database tables using Supabase's auth.uid() function.

5.2 Authentication

All access to the Platform requires authentication via Supabase Auth (email/password or Google OAuth). Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) is available and strongly recommended for admin accounts. Passwords are hashed using bcrypt; raw passwords are never stored or logged.

5.3 Access Provisioning and Deprovisioning

New user accounts require email verification. Admin roles are assigned manually by the Platform Admin. On termination of any access relationship, accounts must be deactivated via the Admin Users panel within 24 hours.

5.4 Privileged Access

Supabase service role keys grant unrestricted database access. These keys are stored exclusively in Vercel environment variables, are never committed to version control, and are rotated immediately if exposure is suspected.

6. Cryptography

The Platform employs the following cryptographic controls:

  • Data in transit: TLS 1.2+ enforced by Vercel edge network on all connections. HSTS with 2-year max-age, includeSubDomains, and preload. No HTTP access permitted.
  • Data at rest: Supabase PostgreSQL database encrypted at rest using AES-256. Supabase Storage encrypted at rest.
  • Application-layer encryption: AES-256-GCM available via browser Web Crypto API for end-to-end encryption of highly sensitive vault documents. Keys are generated client-side and never transmitted to the server.
  • Password hashing: bcrypt with appropriate work factor, managed by Supabase Auth.
  • Key management: All cryptographic keys and secrets stored in Vercel environment variables. Never committed to git. Rotated annually or immediately following suspected compromise.

7. Acceptable Use

The Platform is operated solely for the purpose of M&A deal intelligence, pipeline management, and associated business activities of Howard Henry. The following are expressly prohibited:

  • Use of the Platform to process data that is not relevant to legitimate M&A activity
  • Sharing authentication credentials with any third party
  • Circumventing or disabling any security control
  • Storing financial instruments, regulated investment advice, or FCA-regulated products without appropriate authorisation
  • Using the Platform's AI agents to generate content that could constitute a financial promotion under FSMA 2000 s.21

8. Patch and Vulnerability Management

Security patches for all Platform dependencies shall be applied within 14 days of release for critical/high severity vulnerabilities, and within 30 days for medium severity. The following process applies:

  • Run npm audit weekly against the API and frontend dependency tree
  • Review Vercel, Supabase, and Deno runtime release notes monthly
  • Critical CVEs (CVSS ≥ 9.0) to be assessed and patched within 48 hours
  • Patch activity logged in the compliance_log table under framework: Cyber_Essentials

9. Incident Management

All suspected security incidents must be handled in accordance with the Incident Response Playbook (SOV-IRP-001). Key obligations:

  • Personal data breaches must be assessed within 24 hours of discovery
  • Reportable breaches must be notified to the ICO within 72 hours of discovery (UK GDPR Article 33)
  • Affected data subjects must be notified without undue delay if the breach is likely to result in high risk (UK GDPR Article 34)
  • All incidents must be logged in the compliance_log table and a post-mortem completed within 5 business days

Refer to SOV-IRP-001 Incident Response Playbook for full procedures.

10. Business Continuity

The Platform shall maintain business continuity arrangements to ensure service restoration within defined objectives:

  • Recovery Time Objective (RTO): 4 hours for all critical services
  • Recovery Point Objective (RPO): 1 hour (Supabase Point-in-Time Recovery)

Refer to SOV-BCP-001 Business Continuity Plan for full procedures.
